## 12-Hour Intensive Preparation Schedule

To ensure peak performance, this schedule balances active learning with necessary rest. It assumes a 12-hour window including a standard sleep cycle.

### Phase 1: Foundations and Strategy (Hours 1–2)

The initial phase focuses on articulating your design philosophy. You should be prepared to explain the difference between **User Interface (UI)** and **User Experience (UX)** using professional terminology. Focus on the **Design Thinking Process**: Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test. Research suggests that recruiters value candidates who can explain *why* they made a design choice rather than just *how* they made it [6] [7].

### Phase 2: Technical Mastery and Portfolio (Hours 3–4)

During this period, you should revisit your **Figma** workflow. Ensure you are comfortable with **Auto Layout**, **Components**, and **Prototyping**, as these are industry standards for efficient design [8]. Even if you do not have a formal portfolio, prepare to walk through a design exercise. Use this time to refine one specific project or exercise that demonstrates your ability to solve a user problem through a clean interface [9].

### Phase 3: Visual Design and AI Integration (Hours 5–6)

Leverage your strength in **color palettes** by reviewing color theory and accessibility. You should be able to explain your choice of primary, secondary, and accent colors based on **color psychology** and the **60-30-10 rule** [10]. Additionally, prepare to discuss **Google Stitch**. As an AI-powered UI generation tool, you should highlight how you use it for rapid ideation while maintaining human-centric design oversight [11] [12].

### Phase 4: Rest and Recovery (Hours 7–10)

Scientific studies consistently show that sleep is vital for cognitive function and memory consolidation. Aim for at least **7 to 8 hours of sleep** to ensure you are alert and articulate during your interview. A well-rested mind is better at problem-solving and handling unexpected questions [13].

### Phase 5: Final Review and Mental Prep (Hours 11–12)

In the final hours before the interview, perform a light review of your notes. Practice your "Elevator Pitch"—a 60-second introduction of yourself, your skills, and why you are excited about this internship. Ensure your tech setup (if remote) or your commute (if in-person at Mustang Chowk) is planned to avoid last-minute stress.
